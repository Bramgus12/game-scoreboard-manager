# Klaverjas Rules

This document explains the rules of Klaverjas (also known as Klaverjassen), a traditional Dutch trick-taking card game.

Rule baseline: this document is based on the Dutch Wikipedia article for Klaverjassen, covering the standard four-player game and the two main rule variants (Amsterdams and Rotterdams).

## Overview

Klaverjas is a trick-taking card game for four players in two teams. Partners sit across from each other. The goal is to score more points than the opposing team by winning tricks containing high-value cards and by achieving bonus combinations (roem).

## Deck

Klaverjas uses a **piquet deck**: only the 32 highest cards from a standard deck (7, 8, 9, 10, Jack, Queen, King, Ace of each suit). Cards 2 through 6 are not used during play (they may serve as "turn cards" to determine trump).

## Dealing

- Cards are dealt clockwise; each player receives **8 cards**.
- Cards are typically dealt in groups of 3-2-3 or 4-4.
- A full match consists of **16 hands** (4 rounds of dealing per player), or alternatively play continues until one team reaches 1500 points.

## Determining Trump

After dealing, the trump suit must be determined. There are several bidding variants:

### Classic (Draaien)

A card from the unused pile is turned face-up; its suit is the proposed trump. Starting left of the dealer, each player says "play" (speel) or "pass" (pas). If someone plays, they commit their team to scoring more than half the points. If all four players pass, the next card is turned, and so on.

### Utrechts (Verplicht Spelen)

The player to the left of the dealer must choose a trump suit and play. No bidding occurs.

### Leids

Players bid on both suit and level, similar to Bridge. Suits are ranked (high to low): spades, hearts, diamonds, clubs. Higher bids raise the point threshold needed to avoid going "nat" (wet). This variant includes advanced features like "strik" (doubling) and is always played with Rotterdams rules.

## Card Values

Card values differ between trump and non-trump suits:

### Trump Cards

| Card       | Points |
| ---------- | ------ |
| Jack (Jas) | 20     |
| Nine (Nel) | 14     |
| Ace        | 11     |
| Ten        | 10     |
| King       | 4      |
| Queen      | 3      |
| Eight      | 0      |
| Seven      | 0      |

### Non-Trump Cards

| Card  | Points |
| ----- | ------ |
| Ace   | 11     |
| Ten   | 10     |
| King  | 4      |
| Queen | 3      |
| Jack  | 2      |
| Nine  | 0      |
| Eight | 0      |
| Seven | 0      |

**Total points in a game: 162** (62 from trump cards + 3 x 30 from non-trump suits + 10 for the last trick).

## Playing a Hand

- The player to the left of the dealer leads the first trick.
- Play proceeds clockwise; each player plays one card per trick.
- The winner of a trick leads the next one.
- There are 8 tricks per hand.

## Following Suit and Trump Obligation (Troefplicht)

You must always follow the suit that was led if you can. If you cannot follow suit, the trump obligation rules apply. This is where Amsterdams and Rotterdams differ.

### When a Non-Trump Suit Is Led

| Priority | Amsterdams                                                             | Rotterdams                           |
| -------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| 1        | Follow suit                                                            | Follow suit                          |
| 2        | Trump or overtrump (not required if your partner is winning the trick) | Trump or overtrump (always required) |
| 3        | Play any other suit                                                    | Undertrump                           |
| 4        | Undertrump                                                             | Play any other suit                  |

### When Trump Is Led

These rules are the same for both variants:

1. Follow suit and overtrump if possible.
2. Follow suit and undertrump (only if you cannot overtrump).
3. Play any other suit (only if you have no trump).

## Amsterdams vs. Rotterdams — Key Difference

The core difference is about what happens when your partner ("maat") is currently winning the trick and you cannot follow suit:

- **Amsterdams**: You are **not required** to trump if your partner's card is the highest in the trick so far. You may freely discard a card from another suit. This encourages more teamwork and signaling between partners.

- **Rotterdams**: You **must always** trump (or overtrump) when you cannot follow suit, regardless of whether your partner is winning. Only if you have no trump at all may you play another suit.

In Amsterdams, undertrumping is a last resort (only when you have nothing but lower trump). In Rotterdams, undertrumping is preferred over discarding a non-trump card.

## Scoring

### Basic Scoring

The team that chose trump ("the playing team") must score **more than half** of the total points (including roem). With 162 base points, that means more than 81 points if no roem is scored.

### Going Nat (Wet)

If the playing team fails to score more than half the total points (base + roem), they go **nat**:

- The playing team scores **0 points**.
- The opposing team receives **all 162 points** plus **all roem** scored by both teams.

### Last Trick Bonus

The team that wins the last (8th) trick receives **10 bonus points**. These are included in the 162 total.

## Roem (Bonus Combinations)

Roem are bonus points earned for specific card combinations played in a single trick:

| Combination                        | Points |
| ---------------------------------- | ------ |
| Three in a row (same suit)         | 20     |
| Four in a row (same suit)          | 50     |
| Four of a kind (10, J, Q, K, or A) | 100    |
| Four Jacks                         | 200    |
| Stuk (King + Queen of trump)       | 20     |

**Notes on roem:**

- For sequences (three/four in a row), the standard card order applies: 7-8-9-10-J-Q-K-A.
- If stuk is part of a sequence, both bonuses stack (e.g., a four-in-a-row including King and Queen of trump = 50 + 20 = 70).
- Roem counts toward determining whether the playing team goes nat. The playing team must have more than half of (base points + all roem).

## Pit (March)

- **Pit (Doormars)**: If the playing team wins all 8 tricks, they receive **100 extra roem**.
- **Tegenpit (Counter-march)**: If the opposing team wins all 8 tricks, the playing team goes nat. Some regional variants award 100 or more extra points to the opposing team.

## Errors (Verzaken)

Making an illegal play (failing to follow suit, failing to trump when required, undertrumping when not allowed) is called "verzaken." Penalties vary but commonly the offending team's hand is declared nat, sometimes with an additional 100 penalty points (262 points against).

## Signaling (Seinen)

In Amsterdams play especially, partners can exchange information through which cards they discard:

- **Low discard** (7, 8, 9): signals strength in that suit (e.g., holding the Ace).
- **High discard** (10, K, Q, J): signals weakness in that suit.
- **Kleintje boer**: Leading a low non-trump card signals holding the trump Jack. Partner should try to win a trick and lead back a high trump (Queen or 10) to set up the Jack.

## Scoring Variants

### Kraken (Doubling)

After trump is chosen, opponents may "kraken" (double the stakes). The playing team can then "rekraken" (quadruple), and so on up to "superkraken" (octuple):

| Action     | Multiplier |
| ---------- | ---------- |
| Normal     | x1         |
| Kraak      | x2         |
| Rekraak    | x4         |
| Superkraak | x8         |

The multiplier applies to the full 162 points plus roem.

### Schoppen Dubbel

When spades is trump, all base card points are doubled (but roem is not). This makes it harder for the playing team to go nat on spades.

### Roem in de Hand

Players may declare roem held in their hand at the start of play. Only the team with the highest individual combination scores their hand roem; the opposing team's hand roem is cancelled.
